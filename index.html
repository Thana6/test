A DAY HERE

What Tuesday Actually Looks Like

Every Smart Kids day follows the same gentle rhythm — here's a typical full-day schedule.

  1. 7:00

    Arrival & Free Play

    Each child is welcomed by name at the door, then settles in with quiet toys while the room fills up.

  2. 8:00

    Morning Snack

    A light, healthy snack and a chance to chat about the day ahead.

  3. 8:30

    Montessori Work Cycle

    Children choose activities freely — puzzles, sensory play, alphabet and number work — with teachers guiding one-on-one.

  4. 10:00

    Outdoor & Movement Time

    Fresh air, group games, and gross-motor play in the outdoor area.

  5. 11:00

    Circle Time & Storytelling

    Songs, rhymes, and a story together as a group — a favourite part of the day.

  6. 12:00

    Lunch

    A balanced, home-style meal served family-style at low tables.

  7. 1:00

    Rest Time

    Younger children nap; older ones enjoy quiet table activities.

  8. 2:30

    Afternoon Snack & Special Class

    Snack, followed by the day's extra-curricular class — chess, art, dance, or robotics depending on the day.

  9. 4:00

    Free Play & Wind Down

    Open play, drawing, and quieter activities as the day winds down.

  10. 6:00

    Pickup

    A quick handover with parents on how the day went.

Guide To Montessori

What is Montessori education?

A child-led approach where your child picks the activity, sets their own pace, and a teacher steps in only to guide — not to direct.

What age group is suitable for preschool?

2.5 to 5 years is the sweet spot, when early language, motor skills, and social confidence develop fastest.

What do children learn?

Letters, numbers, colours, and communication — but just as importantly, patience, sharing, and how to finish what they start.

Is safety important?

It's non-negotiable. CCTV coverage, trained staff, and a secure, child-proofed space at every stage of the day.
